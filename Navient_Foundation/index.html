15 East Coast States sent 13,818 Complaints against Navient Corporation Amidst Student Loan Customer Lawsuits


By Maithili Sule

April 18th 2024

Data collected from 2015 to 2023 shows that east coast states made 13,818 complaints against federal student loan servicer Navient Corporation.

Of the east coast states, Vermont sent the least amount of complaints at 68, while the most complaints were sent by Florida, at 2,197, with New York trailing not too far behind at 2,180 complaints.

54.28% of these complaints were federal student loan servicing, while 24.29% were private student loans and 21.43% of the complaints were connected to non-federal student loans.

The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau sorted complaints into the following categories: “Dealing with your lender or servicer”, “Dealing with my lender or servicer”, “Struggling to repay your loan”, “Can't repay my loan” and “Incorrect information on your report.” Most of the complaints expressed difficulty “Dealing with your lender or servicer,” at 5,045 complaints.

In 2017 the CFPB sued Navient on the basis of its failure to provide ideal help to customers. Navient filed to dismiss this suit, which is still open. In 2018, nine teachers sued Navient on the grounds that Navient misled borrowers from accessing Public Service Loan Forgiveness. Settled in 2020, this required Navient to donate $1.75 million to a nonprofit student counseling organization. Borrowers received no compensation from the suit.

Navient continues to vehemently deny the allegations set forth in the lawsuits.
